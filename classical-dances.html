Classical Dances

Kuchipudi

Kuchipudi is the most renowned classical dance form originating from a village named Kuchipudi in the Krishna district of Andhra Pradesh. It is a dance-drama performance with its roots in the ancient Hindu Sanskrit text of Natya Shastra.

The performance begins with an invocation to God, followed by nritta (pure dance), nritya (expressive dance), and natya (drama). It is known for its fast rhythms, fluid movements, and strong dramatic character. A unique feature of Kuchipudi is the 'Tarangam', where the dancer performs on the edges of a brass plate, often balancing a pitcher of water on their head.
